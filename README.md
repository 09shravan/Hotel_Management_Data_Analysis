# Hotel Booking Demand Analysis

## Overview
This project analyzes a dataset of 119,390 hotel booking records from a City Hotel and a Resort Hotel. It explores booking patterns, cancellation behavior, pricing, seasonality, market segments, and guest demographics to support hotel management and sales decision-making.

## Description
This dataset enables analysis of hotel booking trends across City and Resort hotels, examining cancellation rates, average daily rates, seasonal demand patterns, market segments, and customer behavior. It supports sales strategy decisions through insights on pricing, booking channels, lead times, and revenue optimization opportunities.

## Files
- `hotel_bookings.csv` – Raw booking-level dataset (119,390 rows, 33 columns)
- `hotel_analysis.xlsx` – Workbook containing data, dashboard, and pivot table analysis
- `Hotel_Booking_Analysis_Report.docx` – Full project report with findings and recommendations

## Dataset Columns
Key fields include: hotel type, cancellation status, lead time, arrival date details, length of stay, guest counts (adults/children/babies), meal plan, country, market segment, distribution channel, deposit type, customer type, ADR (average daily rate), special requests, and reservation status.

## Key Findings
- Overall cancellation rate: **37.04%** (City Hotel: 41.73% | Resort Hotel: 27.76%)
- Average Daily Rate (ADR): **$101.83** (City Hotel: $105.30 | Resort Hotel: $94.95)
- Average lead time: **104 days**
- Average length of stay: **3.43 nights**
- Repeat guest rate: **3.19%**
- Top booking channel: **Online TA** (47.3% of all bookings)
- Top source market: **Portugal** (~41% of bookings)
- Peak season: **July–August**; lowest demand: **November–January**

## Recommendations
1. Revise deposit policies (87.7% of bookings currently require no deposit) to reduce cancellations.
2. Apply dynamic seasonal pricing aligned with demand peaks and troughs.
3. Launch loyalty programs to improve repeat guest rate.
4. Investigate causes of higher City Hotel cancellations.
5. Strengthen direct booking channels to reduce OTA dependency.

## Usage
Open `hotel_analysis.xlsx` for raw data, dashboards, and pivot tables, or refer to `Hotel_Booking_Analysis_Report.docx` for the full written analysis and recommendations.
